Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about green living

While green homes may have a higher initial cost, they save significantly on utility bills. Most homeowners recover the investment within 5-7 years through energy savings.

LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) is an internationally recognized certification system that verifies a building's environmental performance and sustainability standards.

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic cells. Excess energy can be stored in batteries or sold back to the grid, making your home energy-independent.

Green homes are designed to minimize maintenance. Solar panels need occasional cleaning, and rainwater systems require regular checks. Overall, maintenance is similar to conventional homes.
